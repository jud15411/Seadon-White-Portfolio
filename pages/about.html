About Me

Artist & Creative Visionary

Seadon White

I work in symbols, thresholds, and the unseen architecture of human experience.

Most of my art is existential in nature, concerned with the tensions between permanence and impermanence, meaning and absurdity, memory and time. I'm less interested in answers than in evoking the questions that live under the surface of ordinary life, and imparting whatever affect those questions may have on the observer.

Whether through surreal forms, stark minimalism, or layered metaphor, my work invites the viewer to confront what's often avoided: grief, transformation, disorientation, euphoria and awe.

I have come to this not through institutions or formal pedigree, but through necessity. As someone shaped early by trauma and guided by relentless inner inquiry, I create because I have to, because meaning must be made or uncovered, not assumed.

Each piece is a fragment of an internal landscape made external. Each series, a small cathedral of thought and sensation. This is not escapism. This is the deep dive.
